Form Design Best Practices

Notes take from article here.


Forms play a vital role in user conversion (getting users to convert from "public" to "consumer"). Simple and short forms are more likely to be completed because they are convenient. A one-column layout offers a downward momentum for users. Dividing a large form into transparent chunks is a good way to decrease cognitive load. Asking easy questions first and higher effort questions later helps to reduce form abandonment.


Guide users through the form in a transparent manner to encourage them to finish the form. This includes clearly indicating which fields are required, having guides for formatting requirements, and labels. Labels close to the input field are more helpful than arbitrary labels. Sizing fields in proportion to their input is a subtle, but effective, way to guide users through a form. Minimizing the extra clicks that come with a dropdown menu will result in faster form completion.
